I am a second year PhD student at the University of Sydney and CSIRO Astronomy and Space Science. My research interests focus on gamma-ray bursts, radio transients and astrophysical transient phenomena, more generally. These often provide a window into studying physics in the most extreme environments of our Universe. I enjoy experimenting with new methods to do things and taking a data-driven approach to solving problems.

I also teach data science, computational science and analytics courses for physics, astronomy and business students. I'm always looking for new ways to improve the learning experience for students and welcome any suggestions.

If you would like to get in touch, the easiest way is to shoot me an email!
